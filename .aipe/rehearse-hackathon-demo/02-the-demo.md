# Chapter 2 — The Demo   (1:00–6:00, 5 min)

## Opening hook

This is the chapter. It owns half your slot and it carries the one moment the
whole presentation exists to deliver: the route **bending around a steep red
block** to stay flat, with the card showing only a small climb. That's your
money shot, and it lands by 3:00 — inside the first third — because a demo that
buries the wow in minute eight has already lost.

Your job here is choreography, not narration. The hands do the clicking; your
mouth speaks *value*. The most common way this chapter dies is the presenter
saying "now I'm tapping the To field, now I'm hitting Route, now it's
loading…" — the room can see your thumb. They don't need play-by-play; they need
you telling them what they're about to care about.

## The time-budget bar

You own five minutes, the largest slice. The money shot lands at ~2:45 — that's
non-negotiable.

## The body — the beats in order

### Beat 1 — Type and Route (1:00–1:30)

The addresses are already in from the cold open. You're confirming, not typing
from scratch — typing two full addresses live eats forty seconds you don't have.
Speak the *why* of the pair while you tap.

```
  SHOW (on screen)               SAY (out loud)
  ─────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────────────
  AddressBar with From + To      "I want to get from here to a spot
  already filled                  on the far side of a hill — the
                                  kind of trip that wrecks your legs
                                  on a normal map."
  tap [ Route ▸ ]                "One tap."
```

### Beat 2 — The route draws, mostly green (1:30–2:30)

The colored line appears. The route line is colored by *directed* grade — green
where you're going flat or downhill, warmer where it climbs — and the
RouteSummaryCard slides up with the distance and the climb. Let it land for a
beat before you talk over it.

```
  SHOW (on screen)               SAY (out loud)
  ─────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────────────
  colored route line appears,    "There it is — and it's green
  mostly green                    almost the whole way. Green means
                                  flat."
  RouteSummaryCard: "Flattest     "It found me a route that's one-
  available · 1.4 km · +9 m       point-four kilometers and only
  climb"                          NINE meters of total climb."
```

### Beat 3 — The bend (2:30–2:45)

Now you set up the money shot. Trace the route line with your finger and name
what it's doing: it isn't going straight. It's bowing around something. Don't say
what yet — make them want the reveal.

```
  SHOW (on screen)               SAY (out loud)
  ─────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────────────
  finger traces the route line,  "But look — it didn't go STRAIGHT.
  pointing at the curve where     It bends, right here, all the way
  it bows out                     around something. Let me show you
                                  what it's avoiding."
```

### Beat 4 — ★ THE MONEY SHOT — reveal the red (2:45–3:15)

Tap the **Grades** toggle (top-right). The grade heatmap paints the streets —
and the steep block the route bent around lights up **red**. Now the room sees
*why* the line bowed: there was a wall of hill there, and flattr went around it
to keep your climb at nine meters. This is the "oh." Let it breathe. Don't talk
over the reveal — tap, pause one full second, then land the line.

```
  SHOW (on screen)               SAY (out loud)
  ─────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────────────
  tap [ Grades ] toggle          ( tap, then SILENCE for 1 second )
  the heatmap paints; the
  avoided block glows RED         "…THAT. That red block is a steep
  while the route stays on        hill. The route went around it to
  green streets                   keep me flat — nine meters of climb
                                  instead of a wall."   ← MONEY SHOT
```

```
  ┃ "That red block is a steep hill — and the route went around it
  ┃  to keep me flat. Nine meters of climb instead of a wall."
```

That sentence, on that reveal, at 2:45, is the entire demo. If everything after
this breaks, you've already won the room.

### Beat 5 — Flip the knob, re-route live (3:15–4:15)

Now prove it's a real engine, not a canned screenshot. The GradeSlider sets your
max tolerable uphill grade (`userMax`). Tap **🏔️ 15% (Any)** — you've just told
flattr you'll climb anything. The route **recomputes live**: now it's allowed
over the hill, so it takes the shorter, steeper straight line, and the card's
climb number jumps. Then flip back to **🚶 8%** and watch it bend around again.

```
  SHOW (on screen)               SAY (out loud)
  ─────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────────────
  tap GradeSlider '🏔️ 15%'        "Now watch — I'll tell it I don't
                                  care about hills."
  route recomputes; takes the     "It re-routes LIVE. Straight over
  steep straight line; card        the hill now — shorter, but look
  climb number JUMPS up            at the climb jump."
  tap GradeSlider '🚶 8%'          "Back to walking-friendly…"
  route bends around again;        "…and it bends around the hill
  card climb drops to +9 m         again. That one knob is the whole
                                  product."
```

This beat is what separates a live demo from a slideshow. The knob changing the
route in real time is proof the routing is real.

### Beat 6 — The honesty beat (4:15–5:00)

Close the demo by owning a rough edge before anyone asks. The card carries a
"grades approximate" note because the elevation data is coarse (~90 m cells). Name
it in one sentence, with the confidence of someone who shipped under a clock —
then stop. This *builds* credibility; it doesn't cost it.

```
  SHOW (on screen)               SAY (out loud)
  ─────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────────────
  point at the card's            "One honest note — the card says
  "grades approximate" text       'grades approximate.' The
                                  elevation data is coarse, about
                                  90-meter resolution, and I'd rather
                                  show you that than fake precision I
                                  don't have."
```

## IF IT BREAKS

The demo is live, so it has the most failure surface in the book. Two real risks,
two backups. The first is the one to drill.

```
  ╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║ IF IT BREAKS — the route renders all-green (no red)          ║
  ║ Cause: elevation API throttled (429) → flat fallback, so the ║
  ║ steep block never paints red and the bend has no payoff.     ║
  ║ FIX BEFORE STAGE: warm the cache (see 00-overview) — route   ║
  ║ THIS neighborhood beforehand so grades come from disk.       ║
  ║ ON STAGE if it still happens → switch to the recorded clip   ║
  ║ of the colored route + bend. Say: "let me show you a run     ║
  ║ from earlier where the grades are loaded" and keep energy up.║
  ╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

```
  ╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║ IF IT BREAKS — geocode/network fails on Route tap            ║
  ║ Cause: Nominatim lookup times out or the wifi drops.         ║
  ║ Endpoints are pre-filled, so retry Route ONCE. If it fails   ║
  ║ again → recorded clip. Say: "the address lookup's having a   ║
  ║ moment — here's the route it draws" and play the clip.       ║
  ║ Never tap Route a third time live; never apologize twice.    ║
  ╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The rule under both: never freeze, never apologize twice, keep moving. A 20-second
recorded clip of the money shot in your back pocket means the demo can't fully
fail.

## The "tighten it" cut

If you're running long by Beat 5, **cut the re-route-live beat (Beat 5)** — flip
the knob once to 15% and back in five seconds without the full narration, or skip
it entirely. **Floor: the room must see Beat 4 — the route bending around the red
block.** That's the demo's hard floor; everything else is expansion on it. Never
cut Beats 1–4. If you're catastrophically long, also drop Beat 6 (the honesty
note moves to Q&A).
